About AI Sentinel

What is AI Sentinel?

AI Sentinel is an open-source tool that analyzes images and videos to estimate the probability that they were generated by artificial intelligence. It uses multiple forensic analysis techniques to provide a comprehensive assessment.

How It Works

AI Sentinel combines five independent analysis methods, each examining different aspects of the media:

Error Level Analysis (ELA)

Compares compression artifacts across the image. AI-generated content tends to have more uniform error levels than real photographs.

Frequency Domain Analysis

Examines the frequency spectrum using FFT. AI images often lack natural high-frequency detail and may show periodic GAN artifacts.

Statistical Analysis

Analyzes pixel distributions, noise patterns, and color histograms. AI content often has different noise characteristics than camera-captured media.

Metadata Analysis

Inspects EXIF data for camera information, software signatures, and other metadata clues that distinguish AI from real content.

Texture Analysis

Uses Local Binary Patterns to assess texture consistency. AI-generated images often have unnaturally uniform textures.

For videos, additional analysis is performed on temporal consistency and motion patterns between frames.

Important Disclaimer

AI Sentinel provides probabilistic estimates, not definitive verdicts. No detection tool is 100% accurate. Results should be considered as one signal among many when assessing content authenticity. The tool works best as a starting point for further investigation.
